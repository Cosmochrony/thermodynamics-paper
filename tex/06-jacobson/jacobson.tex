\section{Spectral Jacobson derivation}
  \label{sec:spectral-jacobson}

  \subsection{Equilibrium condition}
    \label{subsec:equilibrium-condition}

    Jacobson's derivation identifies Einstein's equation as the condition that the Clausius relation holds for all local
    Rindler horizons:
    \(
    \delta S = \delta Q / T
    \).
    Here we impose the analogous spectral equilibrium requirement:
    for any local horizon patch \(\mathcal{H}\),
    \begin{equation}
      \delta S_\Pi[\mathcal{H}]
      =
      \int_{\mathcal{H}} \beta^{-1}\,\delta E_\Pi ,
      \label{eq:spectral-clausius}
    \end{equation}
    with \(\beta^{-1}\) the local spectral temperature and \(\delta E_\Pi\) the projected energy flux across \(\mathcal{H}\).

  \subsection{Identification of the geometric response}
    \label{subsec:einstein-response}

    The entropy variation \(\delta S_\Pi\) is computed from the metric variation of the renormalized spectral
    functional.
    In the infrared-dominant local sector, the variation yields a term proportional to the Einstein tensor:
    \begin{equation}
      \delta S_\Pi
      \supset
      - \frac{1}{16\pi G}
      \int_M d^4x \sqrt{g}\, G_{\mu\nu}\,\delta g^{\mu\nu},
      \label{eq:deltaS-einstein-sector}
    \end{equation}
    with subleading higher-derivative and non-local contributions suppressed in the weak-curvature regime.

    On the other hand, the energy flux term is fixed by matter through
    \begin{equation}
      \delta E_\Pi[\mathcal{H}] \;\widehat{=}\;
      \int_{\mathcal{H}} T_{\mu\nu}\,\chi^\mu\, d\Sigma^\nu ,
      \label{eq:energy-flux}
    \end{equation}
    where \(\chi^\mu\) is the local horizon generator and \(d\Sigma^\nu\) the induced horizon measure.

    Requiring~\eqref{eq:spectral-clausius} to hold for arbitrary local patches and arbitrary variations then forces the
    equality of the geometric and matter responses.
    This yields the equilibrium condition
    \begin{equation}
      G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\,T_{\mu\nu},
      \label{eq:einstein-equilibrium}
    \end{equation}
    up to a cosmological term and controlled subleading corrections inherited from the higher heat-kernel sectors.
