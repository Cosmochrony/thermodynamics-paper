\section{Parallel with local Unruh temperature}
  \label{sec:unruh-parallel}

  In the Unruh effect, a local Rindler observer associates a temperature to the restriction of the vacuum state
  to a wedge, with \(T\) acting as the conjugate multiplier in the modular Hamiltonian.
  The present construction is structurally analogous, but replaces kinematic acceleration data by local spectral data.

  The field \(\beta(x)\) plays the same logical role: it is the local conjugate parameter that converts an entropy
  variation into an energy variation.
  The curvature correction~\eqref{eq:beta-curvature} may thus be read as a geometric correction to the local
  spectral temperature, in the same sense that acceleration controls the local Unruh temperature.
