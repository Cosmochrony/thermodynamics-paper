\section{Local identity for \(\delta S_\Pi\)}
  \label{sec:deltaS-local-identity}

  Integrating~\eqref{eq:local-first-law-density} on a spatial slice \(\Sigma\) gives the local identity
  \begin{equation}
    \delta S_\Pi = \int_{\Sigma} \beta(x)^{-1}\,\delta E_\Pi(x)\, d^3x ,
  \end{equation}
  which is~\eqref{eq:local-first-law}.
  The content is that \(\delta S_\Pi\) admits a local density representation whose kernel is precisely the inverse
  temperature field derived from spectral locality.

  In practice, \(\delta E_\Pi(x)\) will be instantiated as the local energy flux entering the admissible projected sector.
  In a covariant formulation, one replaces \(\Sigma\) by an appropriate local causal horizon patch and \(d^3x\) by the
  induced measure on that patch.
