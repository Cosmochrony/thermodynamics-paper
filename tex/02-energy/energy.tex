\section{Local spectral energy density}
  \label{sec:local-spectral-energy}

  \subsection{Heat kernel and local spectral densities}
    \label{subsec:heat-kernel-local}

    Let \(A_g\) be a positive Laplace-type operator.
    We denote by \(K(x,y;t)\) its heat kernel,
    \begin{equation}
      (\partial_t + A_g)K(x,y;t)=0,
      \qquad
      K(x,y;0^+) = \delta(x,y).
    \end{equation}
    The diagonal \(K(x,x;t)\) defines a local spectral density at diffusion scale \(t\).
    In \(d=4\), the short-time expansion reads
    \begin{equation}
      K(x,x;t)
      \sim
      \frac{1}{(4\pi t)^2}
      \left(
        a_0(x) + a_2(x)t + a_4(x)t^2 + \ldots
      \right),
      \label{eq:hk-local}
    \end{equation}
    with Seeley--DeWitt coefficients \(a_{2k}(x)\).

    We define the local spectral energy density at scale \(t\) by
    \begin{equation}
      u(x;t) \equiv - \partial_t \log K(x,x;t).
      \label{eq:u-def}
    \end{equation}
    This definition is minimal: it depends only on local spectral data.
    It is also the natural local analogue of the canonical relation \(E=-\partial_\beta \log Z\),
    once \(t\) is interpreted as a spectral coarse-graining parameter.

  \subsection{Curvature expansion of \(u(x;t)\)}
    \label{subsec:u-curvature-expansion}

    Inserting~\eqref{eq:hk-local} into~\eqref{eq:u-def} yields, to leading orders,
    \begin{equation}
      u(x;t)
      =
      \frac{2}{t}
      - \partial_t \log
      \left(
        a_0(x) + a_2(x)t + a_4(x)t^2 + \ldots
      \right).
    \end{equation}
    For the minimal scalar Laplacian, \(a_0(x)=1\) and \(a_2(x)=\frac16 R(x)\).
    Therefore,
    \begin{equation}
      u(x;t)
      =
      \frac{2}{t}
      - \frac{1}{6}R(x) + O(t).
      \label{eq:u-leading}
    \end{equation}
    The key point is structural: the first geometric correction is universally proportional to \(R(x)\),
    with the coefficient \(\frac16\) fixed by the \(a_2\) term.
