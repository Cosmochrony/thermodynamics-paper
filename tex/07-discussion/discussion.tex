\section{Discussion and scope}
  \label{sec:discussion}

  The construction is spectral and local in the infrared, and it makes explicit where corrections enter:
  higher-derivative local curvature terms and non-local form factors correspond to higher Seeley--DeWitt data.
  The temperature field \(\beta(x)\) is not postulated but defined as the local conjugate of admissible projected
  energy variations.
  Curvature enters as the leading geometric correction to \(\beta(x)^{-1}\) through the universal \(a_2\) coefficient.

  In this sense, Einstein's equation appears as a stability and compatibility condition of a locally thermal spectral
  description, rather than as a fundamental dynamical postulate.
